# Design Language

## Foundation

EstateIQ uses a dark-first interface because the product is a financial command center. The canvas should reduce visual noise, support long sessions, and make important numbers, states, and actions easy to scan.

The design should feel premium, calm, and operational. It should not feel playful, decorative, or consumer-social.

## Dark-First Aesthetic

The dark canvas creates focus and establishes seriousness. It also supports layered financial surfaces without making the page feel heavy.

Dark does not mean low contrast. Text, borders, and interactive states must remain readable and accessible.

## Frosted Glass

Frosted glass surfaces are used to create depth without introducing heavy panels. They should suggest a modern SaaS command center while preserving a calm visual hierarchy.

Glass should be used for cards, header surfaces, navigation panels, and high-value content containers. It should not be overused for every minor element.

## Color Philosophy

Cyan is the primary brand color. It represents clarity, focus, and financial visibility.

Emerald represents financial health, success, positive movement, reconciliation, and completed states.

Violet represents AI intelligence, copiloting, automation, and model-assisted reasoning.

Amber represents attention, pending work, incomplete setup, and review states.

Rose represents risk, errors, failed states, urgent exceptions, and destructive outcomes.

These colors are semantic, not decorative. Components should use color to communicate meaning.

## Spacing Philosophy

Spacing should feel measured and intentional. EstateIQ pages need enough breathing room to feel premium, but the product context requires density where financial information is being compared.

Marketing pages can use larger vertical rhythm. Product-like surfaces should be tighter and optimized for scanning.

Avoid crowded stacks and avoid oversized empty space that makes the product feel vague.

## Typography Philosophy

Typography should feel strong, modern, and legible. Headlines should be concise and confident. Body copy should be direct and easy to scan.

Use hierarchy to communicate importance:

- Large type for primary positioning.
- Medium headings for product concepts.
- Smaller, denser text for operational details.

Do not use decorative type treatments as a substitute for clear writing.

## Animation Philosophy

Motion should be subtle and functional. Use it to clarify state changes, menu transitions, hover affordances, and progressive disclosure.

Avoid playful bouncing, excessive parallax, and ornamental movement. EstateIQ handles financial information; motion should increase confidence, not distract from it.

## Grid and Glow

Subtle grid backgrounds can suggest structure, records, and financial systems. Glow effects should be soft and restrained, usually cyan or violet, and should support the focal area rather than dominate the composition.
