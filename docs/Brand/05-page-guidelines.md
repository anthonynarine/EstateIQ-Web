# Page Guidelines

## Purpose

EstateIQ marketing pages should explain the product with a clear narrative. Each page should help independent landlords understand the problem, the category, the product, and the next step.

Pages should avoid generic SaaS section stacking. Every section should answer a specific question in the buyer's mind.

## Recommended Page Structure

Hero

Problem

Solution

Product

Trust

Pricing

FAQ

CTA

## Why This Hierarchy Works

The hero establishes the EstateIQ brand and category. It should make the product name and positioning clear immediately.

The problem section validates the customer's current reality: fragmented records, unclear property performance, scattered documents, and limited time.

The solution section introduces EstateIQ as the financial operating layer, not traditional property management software.

The product section shows how the system works: records, documents, insights, AI, and portfolio-level visibility.

Trust gives the buyer confidence that financial data, privacy, accuracy, and reliability are taken seriously.

Pricing appears after value and trust are established, so the buyer evaluates cost in context.

FAQ resolves objections and clarifies boundaries, especially around AI, accounting, tax, and property management scope.

CTA gives a clear next step without overpromising.

## Page Rules

Every page should reinforce EstateIQ as the product name.

Every page should connect back to the core outcome: understanding what the portfolio earns, spends, and owes.

Avoid adding sections only because competitors have them. If a section does not clarify the product, build trust, or move the visitor toward a decision, omit it.

Use product-specific language. Avoid generic phrases that could belong to any SaaS product.

Prefer fewer, stronger sections over long pages filled with weak claims.

## Content Priorities

First priority: clarity of category and outcome.

Second priority: credibility and specificity.

Third priority: conversion.

Conversion should come from confidence, not pressure.
