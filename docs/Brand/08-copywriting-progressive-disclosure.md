# Copywriting Progressive Disclosure

EstateIQ copy should make powerful financial software easy for small landlords to understand without removing the engineering depth that makes the product trustworthy.

The writing should sound like an expert engineer explaining powerful software clearly to a smart landlord.

It should not sound like a technical whitepaper on every page.

## Framework

Every major public section should follow this order:

### 1. Headline

Simple, outcome-focused, and human.

Lead with what the owner can understand, do, or trust.

### 2. Body

Explain the practical benefit.

Describe what the owner can manage, inspect, connect, ask, review, or trace.

### 3. Technical Layer

Only after the value is clear, explain the architecture.

This is where EstateIQ can introduce ledger-first accounting, document intelligence, grounded AI, DANA, the Gap Tool, analyst tools, deterministic reports, organization isolation, and reviewed deterministic capabilities.

## Example

Avoid leading with:

```text
Deterministic Financial Truth
```

Use:

```text
Know exactly where your money came from.
```

Then explain:

```text
Every charge, payment, expense, and supported document is connected, so your numbers are traceable.
```

Then add the technical layer:

```text
EstateIQ uses a ledger-first model where balances are derived from recorded transactions.
```

## Rules

- Avoid jargon in the first sentence.
- Avoid starting public sections with architecture terms.
- Keep technical terms, but define them.
- Speak to owners first, engineers second.
- Use "grounded AI" only after explaining what it means.
- Use "supported documents" for document Q&A and document intelligence claims.
- Do not claim every document type is supported.
- Do not claim AI is the financial source of truth.
- Say records and confirmed ledger data remain financial truth.
- Say documents are evidence and context.
- Say AI explains records and supported documents.
- Say Gap Tool expansion happens through reviewed deterministic capabilities informed by user questions.
- Avoid "self-training."
- Avoid "autonomous learning."

## Preferred Patterns

Use:

```text
Know what is owed, what was paid, and what still remains.
```

Then:

```text
Charges, payments, and allocations keep every balance traceable.
```

Then:

```text
EstateIQ uses ledger-first accounting so balances are derived from records, not loose status fields.
```

Use:

```text
AI that explains your portfolio, not generic internet answers.
```

Then:

```text
EstateIQ answers from records, reports, and supported documents you manage inside the product.
```

Then:

```text
Analyst tools prepare structured outputs before the AI explains them in natural language.
```

Use:

```text
Reports you can trace back to real records.
```

Then:

```text
Reports summarize rent, balances, expenses, and attention areas from recorded activity.
```

Then:

```text
Reports are deterministic read models over source records.
```

## Terms To Use Carefully

These terms can remain, but should usually appear after the owner benefit is clear:

- deterministic
- ledger-first
- operating system
- capability
- source of truth
- structured truth
- AI-native
- DANA
- Gap Tool
- analyst tools

## Claim Boundaries

Do not imply:

- AI modifies financial records.
- AI autonomously learns.
- AI trains itself.
- Documents are always the source of financial truth.
- Every document type is supported.
- Reports are guaranteed accurate regardless of input data.
- EstateIQ replaces accounting, tax, legal, or professional judgment.

Do say:

- Confirmed records and ledger data remain financial truth.
- Documents are connected evidence and supported context.
- AI explains structured records and supported documents.
- Unsupported questions can become reviewed product signals.
- New coverage must be implemented, tested, and reviewed before becoming part of the AI system.
