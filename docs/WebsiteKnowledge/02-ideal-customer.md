# 02 Ideal Customer

## Primary Customer

EstateIQ is built for independent landlords and small real estate portfolio owners with roughly 1-50 residential units.

They may be self-managing owners, family-run operators, or small teams that manage a portfolio without enterprise back office support.

## Customer Reality

The target customer often works across:

- Bank apps.
- Spreadsheets.
- Receipts.
- Lease documents.
- Text messages.
- Email attachments.
- Manual notes.
- Lightweight property tools.

They are not trying to buy an enterprise property management suite. They need financial clarity, reliable records, and a system that makes portfolio performance easier to understand.

## Jobs To Be Done

Know what I make:

- Net cash flow.
- Building-level profitability.
- Portfolio performance.
- Expected rent versus collected cash.

Know what is owed:

- Open balances.
- Delinquency.
- Aging and payment history.
- Unapplied payments.

Know what I spent:

- Expense categories.
- Building and unit cost burden.
- Vendor history.
- Receipts and supporting documentation.

Prepare for taxes:

- Categorized income and expenses.
- Missing document visibility.
- Accountant-oriented package readiness.
- Export-ready direction without claiming tax compliance.

Make better decisions:

- Understand which buildings need attention.
- See occupancy and payment health.
- Identify gaps in records before they become problems.
- Use AI explanations grounded in real portfolio data.

## Buying Triggers

Good website copy can speak to these moments:

- The owner no longer trusts their spreadsheet.
- Tax season exposes missing receipts and unclear categories.
- A growing portfolio makes property-by-property performance hard to see.
- Rent collection and open balances are difficult to reconcile.
- Documents are scattered across email, folders, and phone photos.
- The owner wants AI help but does not trust AI to invent financial answers.

## Audience Segments

Self-managing landlord:

Needs a clear operating picture without extra staff.

Growing small operator:

Needs more financial discipline as unit count grows.

Family portfolio owner:

Needs shared visibility, record discipline, and clearer reporting.

Owner working with an accountant:

Needs records, categories, documents, and year-end readiness organized before review.

## What To Avoid

Do not write for institutional asset managers, large property management companies, or tenant-first portal buyers as the primary audience.

Do not use hype around passive income, real estate empires, or guaranteed financial outcomes.

Do not imply EstateIQ replaces professional property management, accounting, tax, legal, or banking services.
